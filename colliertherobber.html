Background

Michael Collier or Collier the Robber is a notorisus highwayman and robber in Ireland during the 19th century with many concidring him to be the irish robin hood with how it was said that he would steal from the rich english and landowers and giving to the poor irish farmers. It was said that he travailed across the fingal reigen robbing coahes of the local land owners of the area.

Crimes

One of his most famous crimes was his robbery of the Dublin-Belfast mail coach at Bloody-Hollow on the Great North road between Drumcondra and Swords. Using only some hats and coats he placed them them on the side of the steep-rising out of Bloody-Hollow. As the coach was nearing, Collier leaped out in front of it and shouted "now gentlemen, resistance is useless, my men line the ditches, they only want one word from me and they will riddle you with bullets". The frightened passengers quicly gave up all their money and valuable items to the highwayman.


Reputation

His crimes and adventures left him with a very mixed reputation many of the rich or well off citizens saw him as a criminal while the poor offten saw him as more a robin hood styled hero who stole from the rich. Which ever is the case he went on to become quite famous with his name still being know today as well as having all of his adventuess made into a book

Death

After many years, Collier was eventualy caught and put on trial. His to be sent to the West Indies. In those days, transportation was like a death sentence, as few who were transported ever made the long journey home again. However, Collier made his way back to Ireland and turned his back on his criminal past. He became a publican for a short while, but soon lost all he had through gambling. He died in 1849
