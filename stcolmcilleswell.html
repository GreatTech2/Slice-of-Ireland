Holy Wells

It has been said that there are over 3,000 holy wells located in Ireland however as they are often unmarked on maps and undistinguished by archaeological features, holy wells are a uniquely vulnerable category of ancient sites. They continue to be lost to farming, drainage work, development or neglect.

Background

Swords own well is known as St. Colmcille's Holy Well is one of the most important sites located in the town. This is due to its connection to the founding of the town. Located on Well Road, it is said to have been blessed by the founder of the town Saint Colmcille in the 500s AD and it is said to have healing properties. It is also where the town got its original name that being Sord which was, in fact, the Irish word for “clear” or “pure”. However, as the century past words and languages would change and be misinterpreted which led to the town's name eventually changing to Sword due to how similar the word sounded to Sord.


Myth

An old legend of how the town was founded says that Saint Colmcille after building his monastery he chose the site after jumping from the top of the Round Tower of his monastery and then landed on the spot to where the Well now stands.

Town Growth

Since the well was first blessed, the town has continued to grow around it with many buildings being demolished and rebuild in order to fit the modern world however the well remained the same as it did hundreds of years ago with only a few updates such as the pump that is directly above it to allow easier access to the water.

Hidden

However, due to the expansion of the town, the well is more or less hidden in a corner of the town near the shopping centre for those that are not actively looking for it.

(image sourced from fingal.ie)

Protection

Eventually a small stone building was built around it in order to protect it from weather damage but was since been more or less left alone however in 1991, the Swords Historical Society decided to have it restored and re-dedicated St Colmcille’s Well and have had it maintained it since then.

Water

Original people could freely take the water from the well and then use it inorder to bless their homes and families as well as test the healing properties but eventually, a steel gate leading to the steps was placed in so as the protects it from the threat of vandalism of any kind.
