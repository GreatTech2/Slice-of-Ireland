Origins

Before the 1980's much of the land that makes up the area known as the Ward River Valley Park today was simply considered as ''Common Land'' where the local farmers that lived near the area would use in order to graze there cattle and other such farm animals.

River

The River that is found in the Valley is know as the Ward River and cuts through a portion of the town the part of the river that is in the area is sometimes called the Swords River it has also be given the nickname The Jacko by the locales of the town in the 1960s. In fact for some time the river was an important aspect of the community as it served many different purposes such as how many of the local boy would use if to help learn how to swim. It was also used to help with washing and bathing when other alternatives for water was unavailable until the 1970s when most homes began to have running water built into there homes.


Viscount

The area that makes up much of what is today the Ward River Valley Park was originally own by Robert Molesworth who was the Viscount of Swords in the year 1716 and before that he represented Swords in the Irish House of Commons. When he became the Viscount he decided that he wanted to make improvment to the land would go on to one day be the park that we know.

Features

When travailing through the area you may come upon many different water features in the grounds these where designed and commissioned by Robert Molesworth with the aim of creating parks and gardens that rival those that can be found in England. It has been said that some were even designed by Galilei who was a decedent of Galileo himself.

Trails

When going through the park there are a number of paths to chose from. However if interested in the landmarks in the park there are three heritage trails to fallow. Each one marked with a color, the red walk is the core walk and last 40 min, the yellow walk which is 45 min and finally the white walk takes 2 and half hours. All of which have makers at important locations.

Access

There are many ways to access the Park however there are some that have serting requirements such as how the main entrance to the three trails is not accessible to buggy's however the jacko section is.
