At Slice of Ireland, we are dedicated to the purpose of advertising of the ancient Irish town of Swords in the county of Fingal in order to increase its tourism and attract more people from outside both the county and the country to visit so that they can appreciate the history and beauty of this town.


Landmarks

There are many recognizable landmarks that are located in the town of Swords both modern and historical this include ruins like that of the iconic Swords Castle which many people visit and explore to the likes of the large shopping centre named the Pavilions which is where most people in Swords get there to get there food and other products making it ideal to visit when looking to buy something


History

The Town of Swords has a long and interesting history with the town being over 800 years old this has led to it holding a large amount of historic importance even with the local church even holding the body of the high king of Ireland Brian Boru while it was being transported to Fingal capital for his funeral.

Swords: Founding

The Town of Swords is located in Fingal County in the Republic of Ireland and possesses a long and interesting history, being founded in the year 560 AD over a thousand years ago, originally under the name Sord, the Irish word for "clear" or "pure", by a well-known saint by the name of Colmcille an Irish abbot and missionary born in 521 AD. Starting off as a simple monastic settlement

Swords: Significance

Eventually, it began to grow in influence even serving as the location of High King Brian Boru's wake before being buried in Armagh. Since its founding many historic buildings have been erected that have withstood the tests of time and have thus become iconic to the area like the holy well where the town got its name from.

Swords: Attractions

The town of Swords being as old as it is, possesses a number of attractions from ancient Ruins and old churches that can be over 100 hundred years old with countless amounts of history embedded in their walls just waiting to be discovered to the lush beauty of its parks and the countryside that surrounds it perfect for those that enjoy taking a quiet walk along the rivers and trees and away from the noise of the town especially popular during the warmer and sunnier months of the year spring and summer.
